  • # Overview
  •  
  • You must tell your neighbours if you want to carry out any building work near or on your shared property boundary, or ‘party wall’, in England and Wales.
  • Party walls stand on the land of 2 or more owners and either:
  •  
  • - form part of a building
  • - don’t form part of a building, such as a garden wall (not wooden fences)
  • - do not form part of a building, such as a garden wall (not wooden fences)
  •  
  • Walls on one owner’s land used by other owners (2 or more) to separate their buildings are also party walls.
  •  
  • ##Party structures
  •  
  • Walls on one owner’s land used by other owners (2 or more) to separate their buildings are also party walls.
  • You can also have a ‘party structure’. This could be a floor or other structure that separates buildings or parts of buildings with different owners, eg flats
  • -You can also have a ‘party structure’. This could be a floor or other structure that separates buildings or parts of buildings with different owners, for example flats.
  •  
  • Party wall agreements are different from [planning permission](/planning-permission-england-wales) or [building regulations approval](/building-regulations-approval).
  •  
  • ^There are different rules in [Scotland](http://scotland.shelter.org.uk/get_advice/advice_topics/neighbourhood_issues/boundary_disputes) and [Northern Ireland](http://www.housingadviceni.org/your-neighbourhood/problems-with-neighbours/boundary-disputes.html).^
  • -^There are different [rules in Scotland](http://scotland.shelter.org.uk/get_advice/advice_topics/neighbourhood_issues/boundary_disputes).^
  •  
  • # Work you must tell your neighbour about
  •  
  • - build on or at the boundary of your 2 properties
  • - work on an existing party wall or party structure
  • - dig below and near to the foundation level of their property
